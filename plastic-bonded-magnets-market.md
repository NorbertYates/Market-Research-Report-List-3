<p><p>Plastic Injected Magnets are made by injecting a mixture of magnetic powder and a plastic binder into a mold, resulting in complex shapes and intricate designs. On the other hand, Plastic Pressed Magnets are formed by compressing a mixture of magnetic powder and plastic binder under high pressure, creating a more uniform density. Both types of plastic bonded magnets offer versatility in design, cost-effectiveness, and resistance to corrosion, making them suitable for various applications in industries such as automotive, electronics, and healthcare.</p></p>
