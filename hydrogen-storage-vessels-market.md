<p><p>The hydrogen storage vessels market consists of four types - Type I, Type II, Type III, and Type IV. Type I vessels are made of solid materials and require high pressure for hydrogen storage. Type II vessels are composed of a metal liner encapsulated in a composite material and can withstand higher pressure. Type III vessels have a fully composite structure, making them lightweight and suitable for lower pressure storage. Type IV vessels utilize advanced composites with a polymer liner, offering excellent weight reduction and lower operating pressure.</p></p>
