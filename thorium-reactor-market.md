<p><p>The thorium reactor market consists of various types of reactors. Heavy Water Reactors (PHWRs) use heavy water as a coolant and moderator. High-Temperature Gas-Cooled Reactors (HTRs) operate at high temperatures using helium as a coolant. Boiling (Light) Water Reactors (BWRs) directly produce steam using light water. Pressurized (Light) Water Reactors (PWRs) use pressurized light water as both coolant and moderator. Fast Neutron Reactors (FNRs) use fast neutrons for better fuel efficiency. Molten Salt Reactors (MSRs) use a mixture of molten salts as fuel and coolant. Accelerator Driven Reactors (ADS) use a particle accelerator to provide additional neutrons for the reaction.</p></p>
