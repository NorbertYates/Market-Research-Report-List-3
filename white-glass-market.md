<p><p>The white glass market can be categorized into two types based on porosity. High porosity white glass refers to glass products with a significant number of small pores, which enable it to absorb liquids or gases. This type is commonly used in applications that require filtration or insulation. On the other hand, low porosity white glass has minimal or no pores, making it impermeable to liquids or gases. This type is often utilized in applications that demand strength, such as in the production of windows, mirrors, or automotive glass.</p></p>
