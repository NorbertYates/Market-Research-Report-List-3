<p><p>A Paper Quality Control System (QCS) is an automated system used in the paper industry to monitor, measure, and control the quality of paper during its production process. The QCS market can be categorized into two types: Machine Direction (MD) and Cross-machine Direction (CD) markets. The MD market focuses on measuring and controlling paper properties along the machine's length, ensuring uniformity. The CD market, on the other hand, is concerned with measuring and controlling paper properties across the machine's width, ensuring consistency in the paper's quality. Both markets aim to optimize the production process and enhance the overall paper quality.</p></p>
