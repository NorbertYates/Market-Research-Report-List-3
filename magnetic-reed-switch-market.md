<p><p>A Magnetic Reed Switch is an electro-mechanical switch that uses a magnet to control the flow of current. It consists of two metallic reed elements, which are hermetically sealed within a glass tube. When a magnetic field is applied, the reeds attract each other and the switch closes, allowing the current to flow. When the magnetic field is removed, the reeds separate and the switch opens, interrupting the current.</p><p>The current market for Magnetic Reed Switches is primarily driven by their wide range of applications in various industries such as automotive, consumer electronics, telecommunications, and security systems. <p><p>The Magnetic Reed Switch Market is categorized into three types: Form A, Form B, and Form C. </p><p>- Form A switches, also known as Normally Closed (NC) switches, have a normally closed circuit when no magnetic field is present. Opening the switch requires the presence of a magnetic field.</p><p>- Form B switches, or Normally Open (NO) switches, function in an opposite manner. They have a normally open circuit when no magnetic field is detected, and closing the switch requires the presence of a magnetic field.</p><p>- Form C switches, also called Change Over (CO) switches, have both Normally Open and Normally Closed contacts. They can be used to switch between two separate circuits.</p></p>
