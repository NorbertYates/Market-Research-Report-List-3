<p><p>Diastatic malt extracts are commonly found in two forms: liquid malt extracts and dry malt extracts. Liquid malt extracts are thick syrups created by mashing malted grains and evaporating the liquid content. They are easier to handle and measure, making them preferable for beginner brewers. On the other hand, dry malt extracts are created by spray-drying liquid malt extracts, resulting in a powdered form. They have a longer shelf life and are more convenient for storage. Both types are used as a source of fermentable sugars in beer brewing and as a natural sweetener in cooking and baking.</p></p>
