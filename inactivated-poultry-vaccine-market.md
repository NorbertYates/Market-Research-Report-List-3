<p><p>The inactivated poultry vaccine market consists of two main types: liquid vaccines and freeze-dried vaccines. Liquid vaccines are in a liquid form and require proper storage and handling. They are often used for mass vaccination programs due to their ease of administration. Freeze-dried vaccines are in a dry form and require reconstitution with a diluent before administration. They are typically more stable and have a longer shelf life compared to liquid vaccines. Both types of vaccines are important for protecting poultry against various diseases.</p></p>
