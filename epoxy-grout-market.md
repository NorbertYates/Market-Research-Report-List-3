<p><p>Epoxy grout consists of two main components - resin and hardener. The resin component provides flexibility and adhesion properties, while the hardener component contributes to the strength and durability of the grout. Resin is typically a type of synthetic polymer that acts as a binder, while the hardener is a chemical compound that initiates the curing process and creates a strong, durable bond. The combination of resin and hardener in epoxy grout ensures a long-lasting and resilient finish for various applications.</p></p>
