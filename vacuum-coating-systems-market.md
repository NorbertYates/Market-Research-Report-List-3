<p><p>Vacuum coating systems refer to the equipment used to deposit thin films onto various surfaces through a vacuum environment. There are several types of vacuum coating systems available in the market. Evaporation involves heating the coating material until it evaporates and condenses onto the target surface. Sputtering uses ion bombardment to dislodge coating material from a target and deposit it onto the substrate. Ion plating combines sputtering and ionization processes for enhanced coatings. Chemical vapor deposition (CVD) involves chemical reactions to form thin films. Lastly, the "others" category includes additional techniques such as PECVD and ALD.</p></p>
