<p><p>InGaAs image sensors are categorized into two main types: InGaAs linear image sensors and InGaAs area image sensors. InGaAs linear image sensors capture and convert light into electrical signals along a linear array, suitable for applications like spectroscopy and machine vision. On the other hand, InGaAs area image sensors capture light over a larger area, enabling high-resolution imaging for applications such as surveillance, industrial inspection, and scientific research. Both types utilize indium gallium arsenide (InGaAs) as the sensing material for enhanced performance in the near-infrared spectrum.</p></p>
