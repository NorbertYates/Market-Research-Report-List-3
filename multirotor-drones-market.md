Three-rotor drones, also known as tricopters, offer stability and agility due to their triangular arrangement. Four-rotor drones, commonly referred to as quadcopters, are the most popular and widely used, providing a good balance between stability and maneuverability. Six-rotor drones, or hexacopters, offer superior stability and payload capacity, making them suitable for more demanding applications. Additionally, there are other multirotor drone variants available, each offering unique capabilities to cater to specific needs in industries such as agriculture, cinematography, and surveillance.</p></p>
