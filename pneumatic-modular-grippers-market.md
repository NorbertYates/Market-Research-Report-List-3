Parallel grippers are designed to hold objects between two parallel jaws, providing a firm and secure grip. On the other hand, angular grippers have jaws that can open and close at an angle, allowing them to grip objects with irregular shapes or in confined spaces. These grippers are commonly used in industries such as manufacturing and robotics, where they play a crucial role in handling and manipulating objects efficiently.</p></p>
