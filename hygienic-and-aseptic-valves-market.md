The types include hygienic single seat valves, which are widely used for flow control purposes; hygienic double seat valves, which offer better leak protection; hygienic butterfly valves, suitable for large flow rates; and hygienic control valves, which provide precise control over flow rates. On the other hand, aseptic valves are specially designed to maintain sterility in applications where contamination must be avoided, such as in the production of pharmaceuticals and sterile food products. These valves typically have features such as steam barriers and sterilizable seals.</p></p>
