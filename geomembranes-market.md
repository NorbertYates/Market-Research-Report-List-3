<p><p>Geomembranes are essentially impermeable materials used in various construction and environmental applications. Bituminous geomembranes are made by coating bitumen onto a reinforcement material, providing excellent chemical resistance and durability. On the other hand, thermoplastic geomembranes are manufactured using plastic polymers, making them highly flexible and resistant to UV radiation, punctures, and chemical exposure. Both types of geomembranes are used for containment purposes in applications such as mining, waste management, agriculture, and water storage.</p></p>
