<p><p>Electrochromic storage devices are used to store and release charge depending on the electric field applied. Metal oxides, a type of electrochromic material, undergo reversible redox reactions, allowing them to change colors and store charge. Conducting polymers are another type, which can transfer ions and electrons to store and release charge. Lastly, inorganic non-oxides, such as tungsten trioxide, exhibit excellent electrochromic properties and are used in thin-film electrochromic devices. These different types of materials contribute to the diverse range of electrochromic storage devices available in the market.</p></p>
